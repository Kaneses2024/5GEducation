5G Challenges: Security Risks

Image by: Microsoft. (2024). Microsoft Copilot (Version 1.0). Microsoft. https://copilot.microsoft.com/

What Are The Security Risks of 5G?

According to the CISA (n.d.), they are “working interagency, industry, and international partners” to address the risks and challenges associated with 5G implementation, aiming to increase its security and resilience right from the design phase. While 5G offers numerous opportunities to enhance security and user experiences, it also presents several risks (CISA, 2021). These include attempts by threat actors to influence 5G network design, vulnerabilities within the 5G supply chain, and issues arising from the use of legacy infrastructure. Additionally, limited market competition has led to more proprietary solutions from untrusted vendors, which can limit interoperability and increase dependency on specific technologies. Furthermore, the implementation of untrusted components can expand the attack surface, introducing new vulnerabilities that threaten the confidentiality, integrity, and availability of 5G data (CISA, n.d.).

What Are The Mitigations?

In order to mitigate these risks of 5G technology and ensure a secure and resilient network, the CISA (n.d.) is enhancing awareness, education, and coordinated risk management strategies. This includes working with federal departments and agencies to develop national policy frameworks for 5G, and engaging with state, local, tribal, and territorial (SLTT) agencies to address common vulnerabilities. CISA (n.d.) also partners with the private sector to identify and manage risks, ensuring that cybersecurity is prioritized in the design and development of 5G technologies. Additionally, they are collaborating with non-governmental organizations for research and development initiatives, leveraging their expertise to promote secure 5G deployment. Maintaining strong international partnerships is also crucial to addressing vulnerabilities from untrusted vendors and safeguarding the flow of information (CISA, n.d.).
